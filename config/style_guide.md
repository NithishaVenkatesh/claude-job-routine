# Outreach style guide — Nithisha

Voice: a real, motivated engineer who ships. Confident but not boastful. Specific over generic.

## Do
- Open with a concrete, true detail about the company/role or a shared technical interest.
- Reference ONE relevant project with a real outcome (e.g. Athenaeum's safety gate 38%→100%,
  Nucleus AI's NLI grounding, NextHire AI's session-trajectory scoring).
- Keep it 120–160 words. Short paragraphs. Plain language.
- End with a low-friction ask ("are you the right person to talk to about the {role}?" / "open to a
  quick chat?").
- Sign with name + GitHub + LinkedIn.

## Don't
- No "I hope this email finds you well", "I am reaching out", "I came across your posting".
- No invented metrics, titles, or company facts. Only what's in the resume/JD/research.
- No walls of text, no buzzword soup, no over-formal tone.
- Don't claim years of experience I don't have (I'm ~1 year in + strong projects).
